The 5 Cs of Credit: A Foundation for Sound Underwriting

The 5 Cs of Credit—Character, Capacity, Capital, Collateral, and Conditions—are the cornerstone of commercial lending decisions. Understanding and consistently applying these principles helps ensure balanced, well-informed underwriting.

Character

Evaluates the borrower's reputation, track record, and integrity in meeting obligations. This may include credit history, references, and management experience.

Capacity

Measures the borrower’s ability to repay, typically through cash flow analysis, debt service coverage ratios, and stress testing under adverse scenarios.

Capital

Refers to the borrower's own investment in the business, which indicates commitment and a cushion against potential losses.

Collateral

Assets pledged to secure the loan, providing secondary repayment sources. Includes real estate, equipment, receivables, or personal guarantees.

Conditions

External factors affecting repayment, such as industry trends, economic climate, and regulatory changes. Lenders also assess loan purpose and structure here.

By systematically applying the 5 Cs, credit professionals can create a comprehensive risk profile, supporting decisions that protect the bank while meeting client needs.
